## Description

This commitment covers a **weekly duty rotation**: each week, one team member is responsible for monitoring channels, acknowledging incoming messages, and triaging or routing **feature requests** and **bugs** that arrive ad hoc. **Everyone who is not on duty that week** should have **uninterrupted focus** for larger tasks that need **deeper research** and sustained attention—without a constant stream of channel interruptions.

The duty rotation supports IFT’s strategic objectives by:
- **Responsiveness**: Keeping community and internal stakeholders unblocked when issues surface outside planned project milestones
- **Triage discipline**: Turning informal requests into tracked work (issues, backlog items, or handoffs to the right owner)
- **Deep work protection**: Preserving multi-day focus for complex work by time-boxing duty to weekly rotations
- **Shared ownership**: Balancing operational load across the team through a clear rotation
- **Signal vs noise**: Surfacing recurring themes so they can feed maintenance, roadmap, and product planning

**Scope:**
- **Rotation cadence:** duty **rotates weekly** (calendar handoff at an agreed day/time); primary (and backup if used) are named for each week
- **On duty:** monitor designated channels during that week, acknowledge, classify, and route work
- **Off duty:** prioritize project commitments and **larger tasks** that require **deeper research**; avoid context-switching into channel firefighting except for emergencies or explicit escalation
- **Triage quality:** when something cannot be solved in the moment, capture **overarching scope** (what the request really implies), **possible considerations** (risks, dependencies, alternatives), and **follow-ups** (who should pick it up, next milestone, or backlog link)
- Acknowledge and classify requests (bug vs feature vs question vs duplicate)
- Create or link GitHub issues, assign labels, and escalate when needed
- Coordinate with **maintenance** and **demos** commitments where relevant; duty does not replace dedicated project work but complements it
